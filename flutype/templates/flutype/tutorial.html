How to load a study into the database ?

Here, I will show an example of a study which shows how to prepare the data to upload it into the databse. Im my case the study actually aims to show you how to upload a study with all kinds of measuremnt types. Therefore, I will give you an step by step tutorial. Let's start.

Step 1: Study

We start with selecting an identification string (sid) for the study. I recommend to find a convention for the naming of the studies to not get name conflicts. Since it is an tutorial, I name my study: "170929-tutorial". The task of a study can be described by a comment. The following table shows the description of my study.
sid 170929-tutorial
comment The task of the study is to demonstrate how a study can be uploaded into the database. Therefore, three different types of experiments are included into the study. The aim is to select very differnet kinds of experiments to get an preferably complete demonstartion of what can be uploaded and how.
allright. Now we have defined a study. Let's go ahead and add some measurements to the study.

Step 2: Elisa Measurement

We start with an elisa measurement without any peptides involved. A measurement contains several informations about the measurement. We start of with the most general information, the meta data.

Step 2.1: Measurement Meta

sid 170929-tutorial-elisa-1
batch_sid 12345689
measurement_type elisa
user ssanger
surface_substance No
manufacturer Thermo F96 Maxisorp
Allright, let's go quickly through the table. The sid is now a specific sid of the measurement. Again, make sure there is no name conflict with exisiting sid. Any sids introduced in the following have to be unique! The batch sid refers to the batch of the microwell plate. The measurement type has to be chosen from three choises. elisa, microarray, microwell. Microarray and microwell refer to respectivly to fluoresence measurements. The user can be choosen from the users list . The surface substance refers to the functionalization of the microwell plate. In this measurement no ligands were bound to the surface of the microwell plate. Therefore, also no fictionalization was necessary. We enter an "No", which is different to an empty space. An empty space refers to an unknown value ( NaN ). The manufacturer refers to the manufacturer of the microwell plate. That's it with the meta data of a measurement. Let's continue with the next important measuremnt related information, the ligands and their positions on the microwell plate.

Step 2.2 Measurment Ligands

Let's start with some general information related to ligands. Peptides, antibodies, viruses and any combination of several ligands (complexes) are referred to as ligands. Each of them is an ligand type and has its specific information. A peptide, for example, is described by thr linker,spacer, sequence and c-terminus. Besides the specific information all ligands share the fields sid and comment. However, it might be interesting also to conduct measurements with different concentrations of the ligands, which is not captured by a ligand. Here, the concept of ligand batches comes into play. I ligand batch links a ligand to the following batch related properties: labeling, concentration, buffer, pH, purity, production date. As always, sid have to be defined also for ligand batches. Keep in mind that ligand batches are particular important, because these sid are later on used in the gal files. Ligand batches, can be of 5 different type depending on what kind of ligand they are referenced to, virus batch, peptide batch, antibody batch, complex batch, buffer batch. The buffer batch is a batch without any ligands. The remaining 4 should be self explaining.

Now back to our measurement. In this example we do not immobilize any ligands on the surface. This reduces our work. No, need to define any peptides or peptide batches. However, still we are supposed to deliver a so called gal file, which maps a ligand batch to a location on a microwell plate. Since no ligands are present in any well, we fill all entries in the table with "No". Note that the table has to have the same dimensions as the microwell plate. For microarrays it might be more reasonable to provide a gal file, which is fine too.
